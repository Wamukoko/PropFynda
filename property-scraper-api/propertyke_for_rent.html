Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most affordable areas to rent in Nairobi?+
Budget-friendly rental areas in Nairobi include Umoja, Kayole, Githurai, Zimmerman, Ruai, and Kasarani — with 1-bedroom apartments from KES 8,000/month. Mid-range areas like Ruaka, Rongai, Thika Road, and Syokimau offer 1-bedrooms from KES 15,000–30,000/month.
What is the average rent for a 2-bedroom apartment in Kenya?+
A 2-bedroom apartment in Nairobi averages KES 25,000–65,000/month. Westlands and Kilimani command KES 50,000–120,000/month, while satellite towns like Rongai, Ruaka, and Ngong range from KES 18,000–40,000/month. Mombasa 2-bedrooms average KES 20,000–50,000/month.
How do I find verified rental properties in Kenya?+
On PropertyKE, all listings from identity-verified owners and licensed agencies display a green ✓ VERIFIED badge. Filter by price, bedrooms, and location to narrow your search. Use the map view to verify the property's exact location before scheduling a viewing.
What should I check before signing a rental agreement in Kenya?+
Before signing a lease: verify the landlord's ownership via the Title Deed (request a copy), insist on a written tenancy agreement, confirm utility (water, electricity) arrangements, ensure the deposit is documented and refundable, and tour the unit in person.
